How this build works and how to use it

The generated build is assembled from a tagged pool of every Super, Aspect, Fragment, ability, exotic, mod, artifact perk, and gear set. Each item now carries a weighted tag distribution, so it reads as a tier like "Damage + Health" or "Add Clear hybrid" rather than a flat label, and the engine scores items by how that distribution matches your goals. Items that show up in scraped meta builds get a small nudge, so the human element of how people actually build is folded in without overriding your choices.

The Synergy loops panel is the payoff. Each item also lists what game verbs it produces (orbs, ability energy, scorch, jolt, empower) and consumes, and a loop is counted whenever one item feeds another. A higher synergy score means the build sustains itself, one thing feeding the next, instead of leaning on a single item.

Reading it: the top rows are your subclass loadout (Super, Aspects, Fragments, grenade, melee, class ability, movement, exotic armor and weapon). The Stat priority tells you which Armor 3.0 stats to chase first. Each armor piece lists a full mod set that fills its energy; hover any mod for what it does. The Artifact row names the single artifact to equip and the perks to unlock in order. The Gear set row shows the 2-piece and 4-piece bonuses to aim for.

Using it in game: set your subclass to the listed element, slot the Super, Aspects and Fragments, then equip the exotic. Install the mods piece by piece (Siphon on helmet for orbs, Surge on legs for damage, ability-regen mods on arms and class item). Equip the named artifact and unlock its perks top to bottom. Build toward the stat priority and the 4-piece set bonus. The whole loop is: generate orbs with Siphon, pick them up to refund abilities and feed Surge, use abilities and weapons that match your element so every kill loops back into more uptime and damage.

Note on data: six of the seven artifacts and the armor mod effects are community-sourced for the Monument of Triumph sandbox; the Encrypted Data Disk artifact and subclass icons come from the live Bungie manifest. Verify exact perk wording in game.
